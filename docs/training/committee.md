# Committee Training Plan

*90 minutes self-paced. Required for every committee Chair and Member.*

## Module 1 — Your role (15 min)

**Read**
- [Committee workflow](../user/committee.md)

Understand:
- The platform replaces our previous paper-based or shared-folder committee packet.
- Every decision you record becomes part of the legal credentialing file.
- Minutes attestation is your sign-off that you participated in the meeting.

## Module 2 — The agenda PDF (15 min)

**Practice**
1. Open an agenda PDF from a past sandbox meeting.
2. Navigate via bookmarks.
3. Read one summary sheet. Pay attention to how flags are presented and how committee history is summarized.

**Reflection**
- What do you look for first on a summary sheet?
- Where can you find a provider's history of prior deferrals?

## Module 3 — Running a meeting (in-platform) (30 min)

**Practice**
1. Open a sandbox committee meeting in progress.
2. Walk through one provider. Try each decision button — *Approve*, *Deny*, *Defer* — and see the confirmation dialog. You don't have to finalize.
3. Practice adding chair notes. Notice the PHI guidance hint.
4. Practice recording a non-unanimous vote.

**Key behavior**
- The meeting screen is a live view; another member can scroll ahead but only the Chair's decision is recorded.
- If the meeting is interrupted, you can resume exactly where you left off.
- After finalization, you cannot change a decision; corrections must be recorded in a subsequent meeting.

## Module 4 — Minutes and attestation (15 min)

**Practice**
1. Open a finalized meeting from history.
2. Click the minutes PDF — notice it lists every decision with timestamps.
3. Follow the attestation link (in sandbox, this completes instantly). Observe the audit entry.

## Module 5 — Ad hoc meetings (15 min)

**Practice**
1. Schedule an ad hoc meeting.
2. Add one provider and set the attendees to meet quorum.
3. Run through the decision flow.

## Competency check

Pass rate: 90% on a 10-question assessment. Focus: decision types, minute attestation, when to defer vs. deny, PHI-handling in notes.
